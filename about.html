Yoga Lineages and Practices

At HariOm Yoga Ashram, we honor the rich diversity of yoga traditions. Our goal is to help each student explore yoga in a way that nurtures the body, mind, and spirit.

Our teachings draw from the classical path of Hatha Yoga. Through this practice, students develop strength, balance, and flexibility in a way that harmonizes the body's energies.

For those seeking inner peace and mental clarity, we offer Raja Yoga practices. Known as the "royal path," Raja Yoga focuses on meditation and the cultivation of mindfulness.

We also embrace Bhakti Yoga, a path centered on devotion. Through Bhakti practices, students are encouraged to open their hearts and connect deeply with a sense of love and compassion.

In addition to these classical lineages, we explore modern styles such as Ashtanga and Vinyasa Flow. These dynamic practices combine movement with breath, helping students build awareness, strength, and vitality.

Jnana Yoga, the yoga of wisdom, focuses on self-inquiry and intellectual growth. Practitioners engage in study and contemplation, seeking to understand the nature of reality and the self.

Yes, we have classes for all levels, including beginners.

No, flexibility will improve over time with regular practice. Yoga is for everyone!

How long is a typical yoga class?

Most classes are around 45-60 minutes long.

Do I have to bring my own yoga mat?

No, we have yoga mats available for use.
